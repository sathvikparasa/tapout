We conducted 10 user interviews to understand how real users useed TapOut in the wild. Like previous rounds of interviews, the core problem validated immediately. Nearly every participant had a visceral parking story, and the anxiety of not knowing whether TAPS was around was a consistent theme across all users. Several had stopped driving to campus regularly or defaulted to always paying after a bad ticket experience, which is exactly the defeated behavior TapOut is meant to address.
The value prop landed. Multiple users made real decisions based on the app during the study period, skipping payment on low risk readings or rushing to pay when an alert came in. All 10 participants said they would keep the app on their phone, and a few mentioned sharing it with friends or family unprompted because they saw true value in the purpose.
The biggest barrier to trust was low user count, and this came up across nearly every interview. Users were not skeptical of the concept, they were skeptical of thin data. When participants opened their lot and saw only 2 people checked in, they defaulted to paying anyway. The app only works when enough people are in it together, and right now coverage feels sparse outside of peak lots like the ARC and MU. Several users also could not engage with the app at all because their lots were not supported, which is a retention risk for otherwise willing users.
A few UX issues came up consistently. The "Check Out" label confused multiple users who thought it meant viewing TAPS sightings rather than indicating they were leaving. The OTP flow during onboarding was slow enough that at least one user nearly abandoned the install. Users also expressed interest in historical accuracy data, specifically how often a low risk reading actually means no ticket, as the thing that would move them from occasional to habitual use. One user proposed a ticket refund guarantee as a potential monetization angle, suggesting real willingness to pay once the trust bar is cleared. We already shipped the UI changes and features needed to address these issues. We changed "Check Out" to "Leave" for clarity and added a chat feature to remedy the unsupported lots until we decide to ad it to the main app workflow.
Overall, we were very happy to see that these interviews were generally positive and seeing people in the wild genuinely excited about the idea and downloading it while we were marketing. We hope that as more people join, we will have the data necessary to gain user trust.
